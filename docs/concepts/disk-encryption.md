# Disk Encryption

## What is it?

Disk encryption scrambles all data on your drive so it cannot be read if your device is lost, stolen, or physically accessed without your password.

## Why it matters

Without encryption, anyone who steals your laptop or accesses your removed hard drive can read all your files, passwords, emails, and personal data—even if you have a login password.

## When to update

Enable immediately on laptops, tablets, and any portable device. Essential for devices containing sensitive data.

## When to skip

Only skip on desktop computers that never leave a secure location. Even then, encryption is recommended.

## How to fix

- **Windows**: Enable BitLocker (Pro/Enterprise) or Device Encryption (Home) in Settings → Privacy & security → Device encryption
- **macOS**: Turn on FileVault in System Preferences → Security & Privacy → FileVault
- **Linux**: Use LUKS during OS installation or configure with cryptsetup command

## Risks

Lost/stolen devices expose all personal data, passwords, financial info, and work documents to thieves. Identity theft, corporate espionage, and blackmail are common outcomes.
